GREEN ANACONDA

Class: Reptilia - Type: Reptiles
The green anaconda, also known as the giant emerald anaconda, common anaconda, common water boa or sucuri, is a boa species found in South America. It is the heaviest and one of the longest known extant snake species. Like all boas, it is a non-venomous constrictor.

LEMUR

Class: Mammalia - Type: Mammal
Lemurs are wet-nosed primates of the superfamily Lemuroidea, divided into 8 families and consisting of 15 genera and around 100 existing species. They are endemic to the island of Madagascar. Most existing lemurs are small, have a pointed snout, large eyes, and a long tail.

LION

Class: Mammalia - Type: Mammal
The lion is a large cat of the genus Panthera native to Africa and India. It has a muscular, broad-chested body; short, rounded head; round ears; and a hairy tuft at the end of its tail. It is sexually dimorphic; adult male lions are larger than females and have a prominent mane.

EAGLE

Class: Aves - Type: Bird
Eagle is the common name for many large birds of prey of the family Accipitridae. Eagles belong to several groups of genera, some of which are closely related. Most of the 68 species of eagle are from Eurasia and Africa.

ELEPHANT

Class: Mammalia - Type: Mammal
Elephants are the largest land mammals on earth and have distinctly massive bodies, large ears, and long trunks. They use their trunks to pick up objects, trumpet warnings, greet other elephants, or suck up water for drinking or bathing, among other uses.

DOLPHIN

Class: Cetacea - Type: Mammaml
A dolphin is an aquatic mammal within the infraorder Cetacea. Dolphin species belong to the families Delphinidae, Platanistidae, Iniidae, Pontoporiidae, and the extinct Lipotidae. There are 40 extant species named as dolphins.

SEA TURTLE

Class: Reptilia - Type: Reptiles
Sea turtles, sometimes called marine turtles, are reptiles of the order Testudines and of the suborder Cryptodira. The seven existing species of sea turtles are the flatback, green, hawksbill, leatherback, loggerhead, Kemp's ridley, and olive ridley sea turtles.

TURTOISE

Class: Reptilia - Type: Reptiles
Tortoises are reptiles of the family Testudinidae of the order Testudines. Like other turtles, tortoises have a shell to protect from predation and other threats.

SHARK

Class: Chondrichthyes - Type: Fish
The Sharks are creatures that appear in the Sonic the Hedgehog comic series and its spin-offs published by Archie Comics. They are a violent alien race from the future Mobius.

RED PANDA

Class: Mammalia - Type: Mammal
Red pandas spend up to 13 hours a day foraging for bamboo, eating only the youngest, most tender leaves.

INDIAN PEAFOWL

Class: Aves - Type: Bird
The Indian peafowl, also known as the common peafowl, and blue peafowl, is a peafowl species native to the Indian subcontinent. It has been introduced to many other countries.

CHAMELEON

Class: Reptilia - Type: Reptiles
Chameleons or chamaeleons are best known for their distinct range of colors, being capable of shifting to different hues and degrees of brightness.

SEA LION

Class: Mammalia - Type: Mammal
Sea lions are pinnipeds characterized by external ear flaps, long foreflippers, the ability to walk on all fours, short and thick hair, and a big chest and belly.

LAMA GLAMA

Class: Mammalia - Type: Mammal
The llama is a domesticated South American camelid, widely used as a meat and pack animal by Andean cultures since the Pre-Columbian era.Their wool is soft and contains only a small amount of lanolin.
